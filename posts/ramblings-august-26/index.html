The watercolor above will be soon heading south, to South Louisiana, that is. It will be headed to the home of an old high school friend.

It was only a few years ago that my wife gave me a cheap set of watercolors in a little wooden box as a Christmas gift. I had no formal training. Like most things in my life I would grow into a style by expermentation and studying the works of others. Studying the works of others is a pretty easy thing these days with Instagram, Pintrest, and YouTube to name a few.

I have read a number of classically trained art folks saying something to the effect that drawing / sketching is the gateway to painting. Perhaps they are right. For me, that began as a young boy laying the floor with paper and pencil drawing crickets and other things with my step-dad. I recall in grade school gravitating to the art section in the library. I found myself doodling and imitating sketches I saw in books.
